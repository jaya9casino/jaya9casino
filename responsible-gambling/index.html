Control Tools

Responsible play starts with limits. Set a budget before opening the casino or sportsbook, decide how long the session will last and stop when either limit is reached. Account tools may help, but personal discipline is still the first layer of protection.

  • Deposit limits to control how much money enters the account.
  • Session reminders to reduce long, unfocused play.
  • Cooling-off periods when a short break is needed.
  • Self-exclusion options for stronger account restrictions.
  • Support contact when gambling starts causing stress.

Warning Signs

Gambling can become harmful when a player starts chasing losses, hiding activity, borrowing money to continue, ignoring responsibilities or feeling anxious when not playing. These signs should be taken seriously even if the amounts seem small at first.

If play no longer feels optional, stop using promotions and contact support or a responsible-gambling service for help.

When to Get Help

Ask for help when gambling affects sleep, work, study, relationships or personal finances. A break is not a failure; it is a practical step to regain control. Account restrictions can also be requested if the player needs distance from the platform.

For urgent financial or emotional stress, contact local professional support services rather than relying only on casino support.
